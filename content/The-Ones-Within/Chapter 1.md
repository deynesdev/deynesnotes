The alarm jolted me awake at 7:00. I rubbed my dry, sleepy eyes and swung my legs out of bed, the chill of the floor making me wince. Louis was already in the bathroom.

"Good morning, buddy. Found your hamster?" I said, taking toothpaste and putting it on my brush.

“No,” he sighed. “He’s just… gone.”

“Maybe he… you know?” I nodded toward the toilet, toothbrush in my mouth. A tiny tuft of fur floated in the water. Louis didn’t notice.

He looked at me like I was a wild animal. "What are you even saying! He would never do something like that!"

I silently brushed my teeth and left the bathroom.

I went back to my room. It was a little stuffy in there, so I reached for the window handle and opened it slightly. A cool wind brushed my hand and arm; I shivered. My phone lay on my desk, screen down, charging. I picked it up and glanced at the time. "7:11 huh. Guess I've got a little time before my classes start."

I cracked the fridge door. On the top shelf sat a carton of eggs; below it, a stack of ham and a wedge of cheese. My gaze landed on what I really wanted: last night’s leftovers, still in their container.

"Bingo," I said, grinning. "Guess I won't be starving today."

I took the big blue plastic container with my right hand and shut the fridge door with my elbow.

I started eating the buckwheat groats straight from the container. They were cold, bland, but better than nothing. I ate in silence, my mind racing with thoughts.

I needed to check with Jacob about Ashley. It’d be awful if we bought her the same gift. Worse if I got something she already had. I decided I’d ask her best friend, Roxy, once I settled on what to give.

By the time I finished the last of the groats and glanced at the clock, it was **7:23**. Crap. If I didn’t leave in ten minutes, I’d be cutting it close.

I quickly threw on the first T-shirt I could find, pulled on my pants, put on my shoes and jacket, and rushed out of my room.

When I entered the huge, old-looking lecture hall, it was already 7:58. I scanned the students sitting and chatting, and walked quickly to the nearest table-arm chair available. I sat down and put my backpack to the left so Jacob could sit next to me. He's always "right on time," so I often do this for him.

I opened my notebook to review my notes and noticed a girl staring at me with a curious gaze. Her round glasses made her eyes look huge. Short, messy hair framed her face, and the collar of her shirt peeked from a brown cardigan. When I looked back at her, she blushed, quickly turned to her desk, her black hair shifting with her movements, flashing hints of green, and started writing something in her notebook.

"People still use paper?.." I thought.

I looked at the clock. 8:00.

"He'll come in three... two..."

And just as I started saying "one," the big figure appeared in the doorway. I smiled. "Just as I expected."

Jacob looked around the auditorium, panting. He finally spotted me.

"Mark! You have a place for my ass, right, mate?" he said.

"As always," I answered, grabbing my backpack and patting the chair to my right. "Man, I'm sitting, like, right next to the door. How didn't you notice me?"

Jacob sat next to me.

"I woke up 20 minutes ago, bro. My eyes can't see shit. And your dark jacket doesn't help," he said, pointing at it.

He wore a big, orange puffer jacket, black baggy pants, and Jordan 4s, each one bigger than my head. He leaned back in his chair, stroking his beard with his right hand while pulling bubble gum from his left pocket.

"Want some?" he asked.

"Sure."

He handed me two gum pads, and just as I started chewing, the teacher came in.

We looked at each other with our mouths full of gum, trying not to get caught.

When he finally got to his desk and started explaining a new topic, we both exhaled in relief.

I couldn’t focus. Ashley's birthday is tomorrow, and I had no idea what to bring her! Ashley, who I’d had a crush on for three years, invites me to her 19th birthday party, and somehow I forgot. It can’t be real… I just-

"Mr. Carter!" The teacher’s loud voice snapped me out of my thoughts.

"Y-yeah?" I said nervously.

"What command do you use to list the contents of a directory in Linux?"

"Uhhhh… It’s…" I didn’t know the answer. My heart hammered; my palms were slick.

I noticed the girl with the notebook shaking her head. _She wrote something on paper._

And just as the teacher opened his mouth—

"LS! It's 'ls'," I shouted, hoping she was right.

"That's correct. It would be better if you could think a bit faster, but for a first-year, it's acceptable. You may sit." His bitter remark was unnecessary, but at least I didn’t get a "minus."

I sat back down and smiled at the girl. She smiled back, blushed again, and turned her head away.

The rest of the class went by pretty fast. I was trying to focus on what the teacher was saying, but Ashley never left my mind. 

I've had a crush on her since I remember myself. There were girls in my life who were genuinely interested in me, but I didn't care. I only loved Ashley. 

But can you even call it love? I didn't actually know her very much. We were in the same kindergarten, then - the same school. We spent some time together, but she always was the "popular" one, and I never actually got into her circle of close friends. But still, every second I've spent with her felt magnificent, and I remember each and every one of these moments. The time when I bought her an ice cream and she smiled to me and hugged me, and the day when it was very rainy and I gave her my umbrella. That day I came home all wet, and I then needed to buy new books for school, because mine got wet in the rain too. But it **was** worth it for me. Because I saw her smile.

And a week ago, when I was looking through my mail and saw an invitation to her birthday party I couldn't believe my eyes. I was staring at the envelope for what felt like ages. I was ecstatic.

The sound of people talking stopped my train of thought. I looked up and saw that half of the students have already left the classroom.

"We should get going man", said Jacob.

"Yeah"

I packed my belongings and left the room after Jacob. Right before we left the room he looked at me and said:

"You know, this one girl was staring at you all the time"

"Huh? Which one?"

"That one"

He pointed at the girl I noticed earlier.

"She might just be into you! Go grab her number or something, man". Jacob gave me a playful punch on the shoulder.

"You remember about Ashley, right?". I smirked at him and walked out of the classroom.

"Bro, she doesn't even talk to you? And this one is definitely interested!", said Jacob, gesturing wildly.

I ignored his words and continued moving to the stairs that led to the exit of the faculty.

"Mark, can you wait here for a second? I need to hit the bathroom"

"Sure thing", I answered.

Jacob walked towards the restrooms, almost opened one on the left and then looked on the sign.

"Shit!"

I smiled.

I started walking around to pass the time. Many thoughts about Ashley and her birthday present were crossing my mind. I still didn't know what to buy for her. 

"Maybe I shouldn't *buy* her something, but instead *make* her something?", I  thought.

I then heard an uncertain but beautiful female voice right behind me.

"H-hi Mark!"

I turned around and saw that girl, who was looking at me before.

I noticed some things that I didn't see before when I saw her in the classroom. Tips of her hair were green, just like her nails and eyes. She also had golden earrings shaped like leaves.

"I'm Iris. Nice to meet you". She smiled timidly, tilting her head to the side as she reached out her hand.

"Oh, nice to meet you too". I shook her hand. "Thanks for your help earlier. You really saved me there"

"I-it's nothing, really...". She blushed and started twirling her hair with her left hand. 

"S-so, I wanted to ask y-"

The bathroom door opens and Jacob comes out, cutting Iris off.

"Yo man, I'm ready, let's go"

"Yeah, just give me a second here". I look back at Iris. "So, what did you want to ask?"

"Oh, it's not urgent. I think you have more important things to do now", she answered, tilted her body to the side and looked at Jacob who looked like he **really** wants to go.

"Okay then!"

I gave Iris a high five and smiled.

"See ya!"

We turned around and walked away.

Iris looked down and quietly said, "Bye, Mark...".

# Chapter 0


The phone buzzed against my ear.

"You remember Ashley's birthday is tomorrow, right?" Jacob said.

I sat up. "You're telling me this *now*?"

He laughed. "Relax, man. It's in two days."

"Two days? That's still no time!"

"Then get moving. Anyway-nature's calling!"

"You know I can *hear* you're already in the bathroom, right?"

"Later!" The line clicked dead.

I tossed the phone onto the bed and exhaled. Two days. I needed a gift that wasn't just money. She needed money, sure - college drained everyone - but handing her an envelope felt... wrong. Too cold.

Hours bled away while I scrolled through half-baked ideas online. Nothing looked right. At some point I glanced at the clock: just around midnight. Perfect.

I dragged myself to the bathroom. The cabinet door hung open, and my pills were scattered across the counter like violet confetti.

"Louis!" I shouted.

"Is your hamster in its cage?"

"He is not an *it*, he's-" A scream cut him off.

"Guess not," I muttered.

The hamster must've knocked the bottle down. Some of the pills were chipped, one half-dissolved in a puddle of sink water, I sighed, picked up one that looked intact, and dry-swallowed it. Bitter, chalky. Always the same.

Back in bed, I closed my eyes an hoped sleep would hand me a miracle gift idea.

# Chapter 1


The alarm woke me at 7:00. Rubbing my dry, sleepy eyes, I got out of bed and went to the bathroom, where Louis was already.

"Good morning, buddy. Found your hamster?" I said, taking toothpaste and putting it on my brush.

“No,” he sighed. “He’s just… gone.”

"Maybe he… you know?" With the toothbrush in my mouth, I nodded toward the toilet. If you looked closely at the water, you could see a little tuft of fur floating in it, although I don't think Louis noticed it.

Louis looked at me like I was a wild animal. "What are you even saying! He would never do something like that!"

I silently brushed my teeth and left the bathroom. 

I went back to my room. It was a little stuffy in there, so I reached for the window handle and opened it slightly. A cool wind brushed my hand and arm, I shivered. I looked on my desk, my phone laying there, screen down, charging. I picked it up and glanced at the time. "7:11 huh. Guess I've got a little time before my classes start".

I cracked the fridge door. On the top shelf sat a carton of eggs; below it, a stack of ham and a wedge of cheese. My eyes kept drifting down until they stopped on what I really wanted—last night’s leftovers, still in their container.

"Bingo", I said with a smile on my face, "Guess I won't be starving today".

I took the big blue plastic container with my right hand and shut the fridge door with my elbow.

I started eating the buckwheat groats, right from the container. It was  cold and not tasty at all, but I just needed something to get me through the morning. I ate in silence, while my mind rushed with the thoughts.

Today I needed to talk to Jacob and ask what he had planned for Ashley. It would be really awkward if I bought her the same thing as him. Equally awkward if I got something she already had, so I decided I’d also text her best friend, Roxy, once I figured out what I wanted to give.

By the time I consumed the last of the groats and glanced at the clock, it was **7:23**. Crap. If I didn’t leave in ten minutes, I’d be cutting it close.

I quickly threw on the first T-Shirt I could find, pulled on my pants, put on my shoes and jacket, and rushed out of my room.

At the time I entered the huge, old-looking lecture hall, it was already 7:58. I looked at all the students sitting there and chatting, and, with a quick pace, walked to the nearest table arm chair available. I sat there and put my backpack to the left, so that Jacob can sit next to me. He's always "right on time", so I often need to do this for him.

When I grabbed my notebook and opened it to look at my latest notes I noticed a girl, who was looking right at me with a curious gaze. Her eyes appeared bigger, probably because of her round glasses, her short hair was messy, like she just woke up and didn't have any time to properly brush it, but it looked good on her. The collar of her shirt peeked out from beneath the brown cardigan. When I looked back at her she blushed, quickly turned back to her desk, her dark black hair swayed with her head, revealing flashes of green strands, and started writing something in her notebook, seemingly with the "Notepad". 

"Someone still uses it?", I thought. 

I looked at the clock. 8:00.

"He'll come in three... two... "

And just as I started saying "one", the big figure appeared i the doorway. I smiled. "Just as I expected".

Jacob looked around the auditorium, panting. He then finally looked at me.

"Mark! You have a place for my ass, right, mate?", He said

"As always", I answered, grabbing my backpack and patting the chair to my right. "Man, I'm sitting, like, right next to the door. How didn't you notice me?"

Jacob sat next to me and let out a loud "Phew!". "I woke up 20 minutes ago bro, my eyes can't see shit. And your dark jacket doesn't help"

Jacob was wearing a big, orange puffer jacket, black baggy pants and Jordan 4's, each one bigger than my head. He leaned back in his chair and, stroking his beard with his right hand, took bubble gum out of his left pocket with the other.

"Want some?", He asked.

"Sure"

He gave two gum pads to me, and right when I started chewing them the teacher came in.

We looked at each other both of us with the mouth full of gum, trying to not let the teacher see us chewing it.

When he finally got down to his desk and started explaining a new topic we both exhaled with relief and continued on.

I couldn't really focus on the classes. Ashley's birthday is tomorrow and I don't even know what to bring her! Ashley, who I had a crush on for 3 years, invites me to her 19th birthday party and I somehow forget about it. It can't be real.. I just-

"Mr. Carter!". The teacher's loud voice quickly got me out of my thoughts.

"Y-yeah?", I said nervously, looking back at him.

"What command do you use to list the contents of a directory in Linux?"

"Uhhhh... It's..". I didn't know the answer. I started sweating, the worst possible scenarios appearing in my head. It felt like time froze at this moment.

I noticed that the weird girl with the notebook shaking her head. *She wrote something on paper.*

And just when the teacher opened his mouth-

"LS! It's 'ls'", I shouted, hoping the girl was right.

"That's right. It would be better if you could think a bit faster, but for a first-year it's acceptable. You can sit now". His bitter remark was unnecessary, but at least I didn't get a "minus" or something.

I seated myself on the chair again and smiled to the girl. She smiled back, blushed again and turned her head back.

# Iris



### 🌱 Iris’s Visual Design

- **Hair**:  
    Short, a little messy, black with dark green tips → this works well. Green tips can be subtle (maybe only catch the light when she tucks her hair behind her ear). You can play with it symbolically: green = life, renewal, something different bleeding into the ordinary.
    
- **Glasses**:  
    Round glasses make her look thoughtful, maybe bookish. Instead of oversized “anime glasses,” keep them modest so it feels natural. She adjusts them when nervous or thinking.
    
- **Clothes**:
    
    - **Top**: White shirt + soft brown cardigan = great, earthy tones, “flora girl” vibe.
        
    - **Skirt**: Knee-length skirt, maybe muted green or dark grey, so she doesn’t look like she’s in cosplay.
        
    - **Legwear**: Patterned tights or knitted socks → subtle cuteness while being practical in colder weather.
        
    - **Shoes**: Big boots are _perfect._ They contrast with her delicate clothes and make her grounded, sturdy. You want her to feel dependable, not fragile.
        
- **Accessories**:  
    Earrings shaped like leaves or petals. Keep it understated (small silver/bronze leaves, not giant plastic flowers). Could hint at her name without being “on the nose.”
		
	Nails are green.
    

---

### 🌸 Personality Hints

To make Iris interesting, let her differences show through _behavior_, not just fashion:

- She’s observant. While Jacob cracks jokes and Ashley shines socially, Iris notices the small things (like Mark saving a seat for Jacob).
    
- She’s gentle but blunt when it matters. Won’t chatter, but when she speaks, it feels important.
    
- She avoids being the center of attention. That’s why people don’t really know her → but when she finally steps in, her importance surprises.
    
- She’s oddly unshaken by weirdness (later, when she reveals the VR truth, she won’t sound hysterical, just calm and certain).
    

---

### 🎭 Symbolism in Design

- **Brown cardigan + boots** → grounded, earthy.
    
- **Green hair tips + floral earrings** → subtle life-force vibe, like she’s connected to nature, or something “living” even in an artificial VR world.
    
- **Name (Iris)** → flower of hope + messenger of truth.
    

She doesn’t need to scream “mystical,” but everything about her should feel slightly _off-normal_, like you only realize in hindsight that she never fully fit in with the VR world.

---

So: Iris = short messy black hair (green tips), round glasses, earthy clothes, big boots, floral earrings. She’s quiet but grounded, notices details, and has a calming “knowing” vibe.
